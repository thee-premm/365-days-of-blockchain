## Automated Market Makers (AMMs)

Uniswap is an **Automated Market Maker (AMM)**.

An AMM:
- holds reserves of two tokens,
- uses a formula to price trades,
- updates prices after every trade.

The most famous rule:

x × y = k


Where:
- `x` = token A reserve
- `y` = token B reserve
- `k` = constant

Trades don’t change `k`.
They change the **ratio**.

> **Price is not discovered by negotiation.  
It is discovered by balance.**

---

## How a Trade Actually Works

When a trader swaps token A for token B:

1. Token A is added to the pool
2. Token B is removed from the pool
3. The ratio changes
4. The price updates automatically

Large trades cause:
- larger price impact,
- higher slippage.

This discourages manipulation.

## Liquidity Providers (LPs): The New Market Makers

Anyone can become a Liquidity Provider.

LPs:
- deposit equal value of two tokens,
- receive LP tokens as proof,
- earn a share of trading fees.

LPs are not passive.
They take **market risk**.

But they also:
- democratize market making,
- earn yield without permission.

This was unheard of before Uniswap.

---

## Impermanent Loss — The Honest Trade-Off

Providing liquidity has a cost.

If prices move significantly:
- LPs may end up with less value
  than simply holding the tokens.

This is called **impermanent loss**.

Key points:
- it’s not a bug,
- it’s not hidden,
- it’s a trade-off.

Uniswap didn’t promise free yield.
It exposed reality.

That honesty built trust.
